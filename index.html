Reupholstery

Tools List:

  • Pliers
  • Scissors
  • Automatic Stapler
  • Screwdriver

    Materials List:

  • Chair(s) needing new upholstery
  • Upholstery fabric
  • Upholstery staples

    Estimated Cost: $50-$100

    Estimated Time: 4 hours

    Warning:

    Using automatic staplers can be dangerous. These tools should never be pointed at the face or limbs in case of misfire.

    Not all fabric-based furniture is the same, therefore not all upholstery projects will follow the same set of steps. The following instructions function as a beginner-level guide to reupholstering furniture that has removable cushions, such as dining-room chairs and plush benches. One rule of thumb is that furniture with straight lines makes for easier upholstering than anything with dramatic curves. Although some of these techniques and materials can be used to reupholster many other types of furniture, this manual will guide users through the process of reupholstering a dining-room chair.

    Step 1:

    Collect all of the tools and materials needed and lay them out on a flat workspace. Make sure there is an adequate amount of upholstery fabric and staples depending on how many chairs need reupholstered.

    Step 2:

    Remove the seat of the chair that is covered in fabric. Usually this can be done by flipping the chair over and removing screws with a screwdriver from each of the four corners of the chair. Keep these screws close as you will need them to reassemble the chair once the new fabric is attached.

    Step 3:

    Use the pliers to remove the old upholstery staples from the underside of the chair.

    Step 4:

    Take the old layer of fabric off the cushion once all of the staples are pried from the base of the chair.

    Step 5:

    Lay the seat cushion on top of the new fabric, making sure to orient the pattern of fabric in a way that you prefer. This does not need to be done for upholstery fabrics with no pattern.

    Step 6:

    Measure approximately 6 inches from the chair seat in all directions to create an outline of how much new fabric is needed.

    Step 7:

    Cut the new fabric along this outline. The 6 inches of added fabric ensures that the edges of the chair seat will be covered and excess fabric can be hidden underneath the seat of the chair.

    Step 8:

    Place the cut fabric face down on a work surface with the seat cushion face down on top of it. The baseboard of the cushion should be facing upward. The chair seat should be centered on the fabric.

    Step 9:

    Wrap the edge of the fabric onto one side of the chair’s baseboard, assuming the chair has a squarish cushion. Secure the center of this edge with an automatic stapler, making sure to place the staple roughly 1 inch from the edge of the baseboard.

    Step 10:

    Turn the chair seat 180 degrees and stretch the fabric so that it is taught against the chair cushion.

    Step 11:

    Secure the center of this fabric edge to the baseboard using another staple. This is a good time to flip the chair cushion over and make sure the pattern is oriented to your liking.

    Step 12:

    Stretch the fabric and place staples roughly 2 inches apart and 1 inch away from the cushion’s edge. Start in the middle and work toward the corners of each seat using this technique.

    Step 13:

    Give the corners of the cushion a trim before stretching and securing with staples. The corners can create an excess of unneeded fabric.

    Step 14:

    Flip the seat right side up once all the edges of the cushion are covered. Place the seat cushion back into the frame of the chair.

    Step 15:

    Screw the seat cushion back into its original spot on the chair frame.

    Step 16:

    Repeat this process for the amount of chairs that you would like to reupholster.

    Step 17:

    Sit back, literally, and enjoy your handiwork.

    • Refinishing

    Refinishing a piece of furniture is when you apply a new finish to replace an old or worn-out surface. Some refinishing methods require stripping, which involves using a sharp razor to scratch away old finish before applying a new layer. Stripping is a messy and time-consuming process which does not always end well, so this guide will explain how to refinish furniture without needing to strip the furniture. The method used in this guide applies to any wooden piece of furniture that needs revival!

    Note: If you believe you are in possession of a valuable antique piece of furniture, please consult a professional before doing anything on your own.

    Tools List:

    • Paintbrush
    • Razorblade
    • Cloth to wipe with

    Materials List:

    • Run-down wooden piece of furniture
    • Mineral Spirits
    • Soap
    • Epoxy putty
    • Gel stain
    • Wipe-on finish

    Estimated Cost: $50-$100

    Estimated Time: 2-4 hours

    Step 1:

    This step will help you decide what your next steps are. Wipe mineral spirits onto the surface of the furniture. The way the furniture looks now is very similar to how it will look with only a coat of clear finish. If the surface looks good, then all you need to do is clean the surface and apply a wipe-on finish. Otherwise, you will need to take some extra steps. These next few steps will show how to restore your furniture if other measures need to be taken.

    Step 2:

    Clean the furniture well. Soap and water are sufficient to ensure that any old grime or dirt is removed from the wood. You can use a paintbrush to clean any carvings or engravings. Once you are done, rinse the furniture with a sponge and water and then dry it with a clean towel.

    The next few steps explain how to fill any gaps or damages in your furniture. Skip to step 9 if this does not apply to your furniture.

    Step 3:

    If you have any spots where your furniture is missing wood, you can fill the gaps with epoxy putty. Get your hands on Quickwood or Kwikwood brands of epoxy putty.

    Step 4:

    Slice a piece off using a razor blade or utility knife. Make sure to only take as much as you need because the putty dries very fast.

    Step 5:

    Put on gloves and knead it in your hands until the two colors blend and the putty gets sticky. Once the putty is sticky, you have about 5 to 10 minutes before the putty dries.

    Step 6:

    Apply the epoxy to the furniture. Before the putty hardens, smooth the surface of the epoxy using your fingers. Then, use a flat razor edge to flatten the putty level with the rest of the surface.

    Step 7:

    Wait a few hours for the epoxy to fully dry. Then, use sandpaper to carefully sand the epoxy. Be careful not to damage the surrounding finish.

    Step 8:

    Blend the epoxy into the rest of the furniture by painting on gel stain to match the color and pattern of the surrounding surface.

    Step 9:

    Wipe gel stain onto the surface of the furniture using a soft cloth. If you make a mistake or do not like the results, you can easily remove gel stain by wiping the area with a towel soaked in mineral spirits.

    Step 10:

    Let the gel stain dry overnight.

    Step 11:

    Apply wipe-on clear finish onto the furniture using a rag. You can do this by making a swirling motion like you would with car wax. Wipe off any excess finish by going in the direction of the grain.

    Step 12:

    Let the finish dry overnight, and you are done!

    Procedure:

    1. Start by sanding.

    Be sure to start any painting project by sanding your surfaces with sandpaper. Be careful not to gouge the surface, you are just trying to rough up the surface so the primer has something to adhere to.

    2. Remove residue.

    After sanding, wipe down the surface with a tack cloth to remove any residue.

    3. Prime it.

    The next step is to prime using a mini foam roller or a foam brush to get into hard to reach areas. Let the primer dry according to the instructions on the can of primer. Once it's fully dry, sand away any drips with a sanding block, and wipe your furniture down with a tack cloth.

    4. Paint it!

    Use a mini foam roller to apply three thin coats of the paint of your choice. Let the paint dry for six to eight hours in between coats. It's crucial that you sand in between coats if you have any drips or residue on the piece to make sure your final product is perfect. Use the sanding block and a new tack cloth.

    5. Protect it.

    Use a new mini foam roller to apply a thin coat of water-based protectant gloss. Make sure you go over this coat very lightly with the foam roller to smooth any bubbles that occured. Allow the furniture 72 hours for it to dry before using.

    Warnings:

  • Do not use a paper towel, and don't speculate on whether it might be better to use a lint-free cloth.

  • Wait at least 72 hours for it to dry before using; otherwise, it can get sticky.

  • Be careful using the orbit sander, avoid placing your hands near the sanding head.

  • Paint in a well ventilated room or outside to avoid ingesting paint fumes.p>

    Our Commmitment to Inclusivity

    On Earth, each of us contributes a carbon footprint, and we all have a role to play. The state of the environment is the result of our behaviors, our neglect, our material items, how we dispose of these items, and the ways that we courageously share new ways to actively work towards the conservation of the Earth and encourage others to do the same. In turn, our furniture refurbishing manual inspires us to try new things, renovate our run down items into something worth keeping, and be environmentally conscious in a way that is fun and creative. Working to be more environmentally conscious brings us together in ways that help us stand out. Our furniture refurbishing manual empowers all of us to connect, work together, and change the world.

    Our Commmitment to Ethics

    The health of our earth is rapidly declining. When it comes to sustainability efforts, it is now or never. Reducing waste is at the forefront of environmentalist movements; therefore, it is both ethical and important that we don't forget about the effects of furniture waste even though it occurs more infrequently than other types of waste. More specifically, furniture waste adds 12.2 million tonnes of material to American landfills each year, but if more people knew how to refurbish their used furniture we could greatly decrease this ever-growing number.

    Besides learning how to refurbish furniture, there are other ways to help reduce the amount of furniture waste produced each year. One option is donating used furniture to donation centers and thrift stores such as Habitat for Humanity, Goodwill and Salvation Army. If the items you are looking to get rid of are still in good condition, try your luck with online selling forums such as Facebook Marketplace, Ebay, AptDeco, Angie’s List, and Craig’s List. One of the most fulfilling ways to get rid of furniture is as simple as passing an item onto a friend or family member who could use it! All of these options are ethical methods of restoring life into used furniture products that would otherwise be condemned to a landfill.
