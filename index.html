Refinishing

Refinishing a piece of furniture is when you apply a new finish to replace an old or worn-out surface. Some refinishing methods require stripping, which involves using a sharp razor to scratch away old finish before applying a new layer. Stripping is a messy and time-consuming process which does not always end well, so this guide will explain how to refinish furniture without needing to strip the furniture. The method used in this guide applies to any wooden piece of furniture that needs revival!

Note: If you believe you are in possession of a valuable antique piece of furniture, please consult a professional before doing anything on your own.

Tools List:

  • Paintbrush
  • Razorblade
  • Cloth to wipe with

Materials List:

  • Run-down wooden piece of furniture
  • Mineral Spirits
  • Soap
  • Epoxy putty
  • Gel stain
  • Wipe-on finish

Estimated Cost: $50-$100

Estimated Time: 2-4 hours

Step 1:

This step will help you decide what your next steps are. Wipe mineral spirits onto the surface of the furniture. The way the furniture looks now is very similar to how it will look with only a coat of clear finish. If the surface looks good, then all you need to do is clean the surface and apply a wipe-on finish. Otherwise, you will need to take some extra steps. These next few steps will show how to restore your furniture if other measures need to be taken.

Step 2:

Clean the furniture well. Soap and water are sufficient to ensure that any old grime or dirt is removed from the wood. You can use a paintbrush to clean any carvings or engravings. Once you are done, rinse the furniture with a sponge and water and then dry it with a clean towel.

The next few steps explain how to fill any gaps or damages in your furniture. Skip to step 9 if this does not apply to your furniture.

Step 3:

If you have any spots where your furniture is missing wood, you can fill the gaps with epoxy putty. Get your hands on Quickwood or Kwikwood brands of epoxy putty.

Step 4:

Slice a piece off using a razor blade or utility knife. Make sure to only take as much as you need because the putty dries very fast.

Step 5:

Put on gloves and knead it in your hands until the two colors blend and the putty gets sticky. Once the putty is sticky, you have about 5 to 10 minutes before the putty dries.

Step 6:

Apply the epoxy to the furniture. Before the putty hardens, smooth the surface of the epoxy using your fingers. Then, use a flat razor edge to flatten the putty level with the rest of the surface.

Step 7:

Wait a few hours for the epoxy to fully dry. Then, use sandpaper to carefully sand the epoxy. Be careful not to damage the surrounding finish.

Step 8:

Blend the epoxy into the rest of the furniture by painting on gel stain to match the color and pattern of the surrounding surface.

Step 9:

Wipe gel stain onto the surface of the furniture using a soft cloth. If you make a mistake or do not like the results, you can easily remove gel stain by wiping the area with a towel soaked in mineral spirits.

Step 10:

Let the gel stain dry overnight.

Step 11:

Apply wipe-on clear finish onto the furniture using a rag. You can do this by making a swirling motion like you would with car wax. Wipe off any excess finish by going in the direction of the grain.

Step 12:

Let the finish dry overnight, and you are done!

Our Commmitment to Inclusivity

On Earth, each of us contributes a carbon footprint, and we all have a role to play. The state of the environment is the result of our behaviors, our neglect, our material items, how we dispose of these items, and the ways that we courageously share new ways to actively work towards the conservation of the Earth and encourage others to do the same. In turn, our furniture refurbishing manual inspires us to try new things, renovate our run down items into something worth keeping, and be environmentally conscious in a way that is fun and creative. Working to be more environmentally conscious brings us together in ways that help us stand out. Our furniture refurbishing manual empowers all of us to connect, work together, and change the world.
